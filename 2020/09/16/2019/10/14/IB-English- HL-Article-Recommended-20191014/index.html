以下分别是对比第二三段的叙述已相关讨论

Quoted from New York Time

The record medal count — one more than the previous high, held for more than two decades by Vitaly Scherbo of Belarus — strengthened the case of those who already consider Biles, at 22, to be the greatest gymnast of all time.

Biles’s victory in the floor exercise was not a surprise; she had been favored to win. But she jumped out of her seat when she saw her score on the balance beam, which has proved far more challenging for her in recent years. She had to grab the beam to keep her balance in two recent major finals, at the Rio Games and at last year’s worlds, and settled for the bronze each time.

Quoted from Guardian

Soon after securing a convincing victory on the beam in Stuttgart to overtake Belarusian Vitaly Scherbo’s record tally of 23 world medals, the 22-year-old Biles successfully defended her floor title to win medal number 25.

The four-time Olympic champion is now the owner of 19 gold medals across four championships against 12 for Scherbo, who competed in five world events between 1991 and 1996.

Quoted from Reddit

they’re changing the rules to make it harder for her to win lmao.

i.e. she’s hitting never before done moves and they are assigning these new moves lower points values than they should be worth (according to e.g. USA gymnastics).

This is not something new. They’ve done it before more recently with Daiane Santos, way back with Nadia Comăneci and a few others.

When the British cycling team were taking absurd amounts of medals at the Olympics in Beijing, including 8 golds out of a possible 14, they changed the rules to allow only one entry per country, to avoid the Brits destroying everyone else.

Happily, it didn’t work, and the Brits got another 8 golds at London, and dominated completely, and did it again in Rio.

With the Olympic cycling it was financial doping. The UK government wanted medals, so they identified sports where they could get the biggest number of medals without having to have, y’know, the most naturally gifted athletes and where no other country had staked a particular claim. So they isolated cycling because there were absurd numbers of medals up for grabs and no other country was really pushing for it. Then they spent big on coaches, on training, on equipment, and they cleaned up.

The way that Olympic athletes are funded in the UK is pretty cynical. They’re all about the medals, if they think you’re going to win one they’ll support you, but if you’re not considered a likely winner funding can dry up. And I know people will say that’s sort of the point, but they’d be wrong to say that. It’s the Olympics, it’s meant to be good clean fun. You’re supposed to put out a team the country can root for, not just hedge your bets on one minority interest sport because it happens to pay out medals like a generous slot machine.

I don’t know enough about other countries to comment on how they do it, not my pig not my farm, but I’m guessing if you dig deep enough most of the countries around the top end of the medals table are equally cynical (i.e. state sponsored doping programs mentioning no names)..
