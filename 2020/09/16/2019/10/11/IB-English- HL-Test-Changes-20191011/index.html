IB Curriculum and Testing Changes

Quoted from https://theverdictjhs.com/author/theverdictjhs/

Approximately every seven years, the International Baccalaureate Organization rewrites curriculum and testing for various subject areas in order to accommodate feedback from teachers. IB has recently rolled out changes for the 2019-2020 school year that will affect students taking Mathematics, Language and Literature, and Language B.

“These changes are beneficial to students because they are simplifying it for students,” said Justice IB Coordinator Jennifer Kresse Rodriguez.

Language B

There are also major changes to Language B (Spanish, French, and Arabic), language acquisition, testing. The first testing year will be 2020, meaning students who are currently in level one of the language courses will be the first to take the new exams at 2020.

Both SL and HL students will have a paper one, worth 25 percent, a paper two, worth 50 percent, and an internal individual oral which will be worth 25 percent. HL exams will be slightly longer than those SL students will take, but their exams are similar. Paper one is an essay exam, similar to the current paper two.

Paper two, the most heavily weighted component of the exam, is listening and reading comprehension.

fffffffffffffffffffffffffffffThe SL individual oral will be the same as the current oral, based on a picture. The HL individual oral will be a conversation based on a literary work studied in the class.
