IB Curriculum and Testing Changes

​

Quoted from https://theverdictjhs.com/author/theverdictjhs/

Approximately every seven years, the International Baccalaureate Organization rewrites curriculum and testing for various subject areas in order to accommodate feedback from teachers. IB has recently rolled out changes for the 2019-2020 school year that will affect students taking Mathematics, Language and Literature, and Language B.

“These changes are beneficial to students because they are simplifying it for students,” said Justice IB Coordinator Jennifer Kresse Rodriguez.

Language and Literature

In IB Language and Literature, new testing formats will begin at 2020. As with mathematics, students who have already began the course will continue with the current testing format. The first year to take the new exams, which are outlined in a newly published guide, will be 2021.

Currently, in standard level classes there are four assessments contributing to the overall exam score: paper one, paper two, the written assessment, and the individual oral commentary. Students beginning the class 2020r will complete only the paper one, paper two, and individual oral.

The new SL paper one will be worth 35 percent of the final exam grade. Paper one will be a guided literary analysis lasting one hour and fifteen minutes. The student will be given two passages in two literary forms, and they will have to analyze both passages.

**Paper two will also be worth 35 percent lasting one hour and 45 minutes, and will be a comparative essay on two literary works studied in the course. Students will be given four questions and asked to respond to one. **

The final 30 percent of the exam will be the Individual Oral, which is a fifteen minute commentary in which the student must examine the ways in which the global issue of their choice is presented through the context and form of two of the works studied in class.

Thenew higher level testing will **consist of a paper one, worth 35 percent, paper two, worth 25 percent, a higher level essay, worth 20 percent, and an individual oral, worth 20 percent. **

The paper one, paper two, and individual oral are all similar to SL, but they are slightly longer in duration. The higher level essay is an essay on one literary text or work studied during the course. It is similar to the current written assignment
