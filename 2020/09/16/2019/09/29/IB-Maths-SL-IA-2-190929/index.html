Well, if you want to do that topic, it’s not simple.

Data collection is very important for your IA topic.

Analysis of dog movement in a recorded camera is kind of a painful procedure (I have no clue about cheetahs, but I guess you can get it off the internet).

Don’t make it general nor make it too narrow, once someone reads your IA they must feel that you’ve explored the topic through various mathematical techniques, and what will you derive though? the speed of movement or the rate of change in the movement? Also consider whether you’ll do it in the 3D analysis or 2D analysis, because if you choose 3D analysis then for the derivation of multiple variables then you must learn at least partial differentiation.

Honestly speaking,no one finishes their Math IA with what they had in their initial planning. I myself had to change my Math IA 6 different times in order to get what I want.

Sure you may expand it, but you must fit everything into 12 (max 14) pages, excluding bibliography. So choose the best 3 ideas on your IA approach and narrow them down.

Overall, I feel that your math IA is certainly not simple, but in fact, super crazy complicated (will surely take 3-4 weeks). You have a lot of learning and video analysis to do, and make sure you follow the Math SL/HL IA criteria’s to top up the grades.

Enjoy :D
