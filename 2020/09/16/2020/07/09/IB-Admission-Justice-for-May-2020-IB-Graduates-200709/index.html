IB Admission —— Justice for May 2020 IB Graduates (200709)

2020-09-16
2020-07-09

Admission

Justice for May 2020 IB Graduates, Vote

https://www.change.org/p/international-baccalaureate-organisation-ibo-justice-for-may-2020-ib-graduates?recruiter=935852572&recruited_by_id=d8eaf040-2ed8-11e9-9bd9-e5392f097eda&utm_source=share_petition&utm_medium=copylink&utm_campaign=petition_dashboard

Due to the current crisis that has taken the world by storm (the coronavirus) the end of year examinations for May 2020 have been cancelled. Today on the 5th of July, many students got to see their final grades from their IB coordinators and others are still waiting to receive their final grades tomorrow.

So far, what we’ve seen is a great deal of injustice; many students around the world got significantly lower final grades than what they were predicted. For example; 41 down to 34, 43 down to 37, 38 down to 28, 42 down to 36, 30 down to 26 and the list goes on. Essentially, the IB has lowered some students by up to 12 points! This is the first time in the history of IB that we see such substantial differences between the predicted grades and the final grades, on a large scale, considering that the IB examiners had no real grounds to evaluate and assign us our grades other than marking our coursework - on top of that, the IBO decided to take into account the historical data of individual schools (passing/failing rates) along with global data per individual subject. How is that a fair way to assign IB graduates their final grades considering the current circumstances they are living in? How is that fair on the students that have worked for 2 years + to get into the universities they want to study their passion(s)?

As a student myself, I want justice for my fellow students around the world. This is unreasonable coming from the IB which is notorious for preaching educational equality and justice. If no change is made, many students will either result in doing another year of IB or no longer have the motivation to continue studying as a whole. We deserve justice, and we deserve to move on with our lives. Many of us have already suffered too much these past few years and even more so recently, mentally and physically. Many of us were not even given the opportunity to increase our predicted grades when the news prevailed.

One thing I would suggest to the IBO is to take a different approach with their grading algorithm and to make it fairer rather than generalising one part of the world to the next. Rather than brushing aside the predicted grades, take them into account as our teachers are professionally trained and their input matters. Your input is not significantly more important than the teachers who actually guided us for 2 years + through this journey. All this is considering the blatant fact that you, as stated above, have no real grounds to grade us and even more so stretch out our final grades way under our predicted. If the IBO does not make a change, I believe this situation would impose a negative image on their reputation. To summarise, what I am telling you, in plain English is this: grade us again, and this time grade us fairly.

If enough of us unite together, we can make a change and bring justice to all the students around the world.

Help me help you!
