##Guide to the Chemistry IA

Working with IB students for over 20 years, I have witnessed stress and anxiety consume student’s during their Chemistry Internal Assessments. Often, the IA combined with tests, papers, TOK presentations, can feel like the extra bit of water needed to turn a large tidal wave into an insurmountable tsunami wave.

That’s why the best medicine for IA related stress is to be aware of how to write the Chemistry internal assessment in as efficient process as possible. This guide represents the totality of the advice that I have given my own IB Science students when they begin this journey. By the end of this guide, you will have found the direction necessary to move forward, and write a strong IA, as well as answering some of the questions that might be occupying your mind right now:

  • How do I even choose a topic for my Chemistry IA?
  • Why is there so much emphasis on a “focused research” question?
  • In what way do I create a procedure for this investigation I will attempt?
  • What Rubric and Guidelines should I follow for my Chemistry IA?
  • What is the difference between the analysis and conclusion in an IA?
  • What layout should I use for my Chemistry IA?
