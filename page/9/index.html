IB Chemistry SL — Nuclear Atom （190917）关于化学里如何计算分子/原子量等等

Chemistry SL

The Nuclear Atom

Quick question

Quote from Oxford IB Chemistry Course Book (p. 39):

Q1： Deduce the ratio of the mass of oxygen per gram of sulfur in the compounds sulfur dioxide, SO2(g), and sulfur trioxide,SO3(g).

Hint 1

目的是对比 两个化合物 （SO2 或 SO3） 中都有的一项元素 (O) 的 mass 比率( the ratio of the mass), , 所以先要知道mass 比率怎么计算。

Hint 2

关于什么是mass比率，可以用以下例子理解：
考虑测量碳和氧形成两种化合物一氧化碳,CO（g）和二氧化碳，CO 2（g）。一个实验可以测量3克碳与之结合4克氧气形成一氧化碳，而3克碳与8克氧气结合形成二氧化碳。

在这里，碳和氧以不同的比例组合以组合出不同的化合物：

CO（g）：C：O比= 3：4

CO 2（g）：C：O比= 3：8

A1:

Both sulfur dioxide, SO2 (g), and sulfur trioxide, SO3 (g) are acidic oxides (酸性氧化物) with sulfur having a different oxidation numbers

接下来是计算示例：

SO2 SO3
S O S O
Ar 32.07 16.00 32.07 16.00
Combined mass of individual atoms 32.07 (16.00 × 2) = 32.00 32.07 (16.00 × 4)= 48.00
Calculate ratio 32.07/ 32.07 = 1.000 32.00/32.07 = 0.9978 32.07/ 32.07 = 1.000 48.00 /32.07 =1.497
Ratio of the mass of oxygen per gram of sulfur 0.9978: 1.000 (Simplified) 1:1 1.497: 1.000 (Simplified) 1.5:1

Note, In chemistry, the formula weight is a quantity computed by multiplying the atomic weight (in atomic mass units) of each element in a chemical formula by the number of atoms of that element present in the formula, then adding all of these products together

化学里的“重量计算”， 其实是计算分子式里的每个元素的 “原子重量“， 找出每个元素的atomic mass unit， 然后乘以元素的原子个数得出。比如SO2, 就是一个硫两个氧的 总共atomic mass, 就是32.07+ 16.00*2,， 而每个元素的atomic mass unit 都可以在元素周期表里找到

IB Chemistry SL — Stoichiometric（190915）关于水蒸气、开水温度、感觉凉爽与否等等

IB Chemistry SL

Stoichiometric relationships

Activity answers

Q1： Explain why the temperature of a boiling liquid does not increase despite energy being constantly applied

Hint 1

Intermolecular attractions

与分子间的吸引力变强或变弱有关

Hint 2

At high temperature the particles of a liquid move more quickly and the distances between the particles increase. Intermolecular attractions become less significant and eventually the liquid become gas

温度越高，分子间的吸引力就越弱，慢慢液体变气体

A1:

Energy provided to the system is being used to break down the intermolecular hydrogen bonds between the H2O molecules

Q2： Deduce which would be more painful, scalding your skin with water vapour or boiling water

粗看是开水 vs 蒸汽谁更烫的问题？但其实问题是谁的伤害更大，注意这里有一个可能认识不清的小常识错误

Hint 1

Cooling brings about the reverse processes to heating – the condensation of water vapour to form liquid water, and the freezing of liquid water to form a solid

注意，这里的凝结condensation，严格意义上是指从气体到液体。冷冻freezing， 是指从液体到固体

Hint 2

If you heat a block of ice in a beaker it will melt to form liquid water. If you continue heating the water, it will boil to form water vapour.

A2:

Water vapour results in more painful burns to the skin; boiling water and water vapour both have a temperature of 100°C; vaporisation is an endothermic process; condensation of water vapour on the skin is an exothermic process

注意，烧开的水和蒸汽都是100摄氏度。蒸汽烫伤更严重，是因为蒸汽在皮肤上凝结condensation的过程是一个放热exothermic 的过程 (与之相对的，叫吸热endothermic 的过程，在开水变成蒸汽的时候）

Q3： Explain why you might feel cold and shiver when you get out of the water at the beach on a very hot, windy day..

Hint 1

Evaporatoin

A3:

The heat from the sun and warm air passing over the body increases the rate of evaporation from the skin; evaporation is an endothermic process; water molecules leaving the surface of the skin results in a loss of energy from the skin; the response of the human body is to shiver

有意思的是太阳或周遭的热空气蒸发了皮肤上的水分，所以你会觉得凉，这个和一般认为的太阳出来就暖和，热风一吹就不冷，真还有点认识上的区别

Comments

Well, if you want to do that topic, it’s not simple.

Data collection is very important for your IA topic.

Analysis of dog movement in a recorded camera is kind of a painful procedure (I have no clue about cheetahs, but I guess you can get it off the internet).

Don’t make it general nor make it too narrow, once someone reads your IA they must feel that you’ve explored the topic through various mathematical techniques, and what will you derive though? the speed of movement or the rate of change in the movement? Also consider whether you’ll do it in the 3D analysis or 2D analysis, because if you choose 3D analysis then for the derivation of multiple variables then you must learn at least partial differentiation.

Honestly speaking,no one finishes their Math IA with what they had in their initial planning. I myself had to change my Math IA 6 different times in order to get what I want.

Sure you may expand it, but you must fit everything into 12 (max 14) pages, excluding bibliography. So choose the best 3 ideas on your IA approach and narrow them down.

Overall, I feel that your math IA is certainly not simple, but in fact, super crazy complicated (will surely take 3-4 weeks). You have a lot of learning and video analysis to do, and make sure you follow the Math SL/HL IA criteria’s to top up the grades.

Enjoy :D

Maths SL IA —— What matters in your IA report? (190929)

Maths SL

Exploration (IA)

You don’t need to make your IA topic very complicated and innovative in order to achieve a good grade in a subject

Quoted from reddit “PSA: you don’t need to make your IA topic very complicated and innovative…”

I was talking about IAs with some IB students not long ago, and this made me remember something that I wanted to tell to people on this sub for some time now, and something that I wish I could tell to myself and my classmates 1 year ago. Basically, it’s the title: you don’t absolutely have to choose some kind of super interesting and complex topic for your IA if you are aiming for a good grade.

At around the same time one year ago, I was stressing out about my chemistry, biology and mathematics IAs, trying to find some original idea that would blow away the examiner and score me the top mark. A lot of things were happening at that point in time, both in life and in school, that made me feel extremely exhausted and stressed. In the end, I couldn’t be bothered anymore, and settled for something very simple and overdone in every subject. For my chemistry IA, I did a vitamin C titration (as the other 50% of my class), for my biology IA, I did an experiment about the effects of pH on yeast activity, and for my math IA, I modelled an infectious disease using the SIR model. If you have any experience with these subjects and if you have researched IA topics already, you probably know that these 3 topics are probably some of the most overdone topics in IB history. I thought that I was committing a grave mistake and had to give up my hope for those sweet 7s, but I couldn’t really do anything about my chosen topics anymore and had to go with it. In return, I made sure that I made my IAs as good as possible (religiously followed each criterium word by word, followed all the tips I could find on the internet, asked people here and on the discord for help if I was stuck, etc). And finally, I also tried my best to perform well in the exams. In the end, I managed to score 7s in both biology and chemistry HL, and was 1 mark away from a 7 in math SL. And mind you, I was not some kind of top student who scored 100/100 on exams. I performed maybe above average on the exams, and even then, my super basic and overdone IAs did not hinder me from achieving a good grade.

I hope that this does not come out as bragging, because that’s not what I’m trying to achieve here. I just wanted to demonstrate that it’s okay to go for an easy IA topic, perform well in the exams, and still get good grades, without having to put yourself through hell to find this super-amazing-IA-idea-that-will-blow-everyone’s-mind-away. I saw a lot of classmates try to do that and stress themselves out a lot about their IA topic. Eventually, because they would choose an overly complicated topic, it would inevitably fail/they would come over a blunder that was hard to solve, and they had to start over again, which added on to their already stressful lives. Basically, there’s nothing wrong with choosing an easy and overdone topic. If you’re super passionate and interested in an idea, then go for it! But if your schedule is tight and you’re stressed out already, then know that it’s okay to settle for something nice and easy :) Just make sure to choose a topic that yields itself to the criteria easily, and to follow the criteria word by word - then you should not have any problem achieving a good grade!

Comments

Agree. What matters is your report. Having a detailed analysis of your data after processing and evaluating your hypothesis and research question insightfully (for bio: limitations and improvements, inherent weaknesses) is sufficient to perform well in these criteria. Having a concise background with relevant information related to your IA is what your markers look for.

Comments

Way too many people think criteria like personal engagement is based on how interesting/unique the topic of investigation is. This is untrue. Personal engagement is based on how you tailor your experiment and data collection methodology to accommodate whatever resources and equipment you have, and to answer your research question. In other words, explain why you performed the non-standard steps in your procedure, explain difficulties and modifications you made to allow the experiment to work, write as if you didn’t half-ass it (even if you did).

IB Chemistry SL (190923）—— 元素周期表一些提法和注意点

Chemistry SL

The Periodic Table

Study Tips

Quote from The Periodic Table: its story and its significance (p. 10, p. 18):

Tips 1 少数元素用单一字母作为元素符号

In the modern periodic table, a small minority of elements are represented by a single letter of the alphabet. These include hydrogen氢, carbon碳, oxygen氧, nitrogen氮, sulfur硫, and fluorine氟, which appear as H, C, O, N, S, and F.

Tips 2大多数元素用两个字母作为元素符号

Most elements are depicted by two letters, the first of which is a capital letter and the second a lowercase letter. This gives rise to such element symbols as Li, Be, Ne, Ca, and Sc,for lithium锂, beryllium铍, neon氖, calcium钙, and scandium钪, respectively.

Tips 3少数新发现的元素，在其发现者还没有公开确认的情况下，由三个字母表示

For a brief period of time 曾有一个简短的时期, some heavy elements(重元素） were depicted by three letters, but many have now been given particular names, such as seaborgium for element 106. But the very recently discovered elements, such as 112, 114, and 116, whose official discoverers are still under discussion, continue to be depicted by three letters, namely, Uub, Uuq, and Uuh.

在自然界中发现的铀由三种具有相对丰度 relative abundances的同位素组成

Tips 4 传统的元素周期表由行和列组成，通过每行每列都可以观察到一些趋势

The conventional periodic table consists of rows and columns. Trends can be observed among the elements going across and down the table. Each horizontal row represents a single period of the table.

On crossing a period, one passes from metals such as potassium and calcium on the left, through transition metals such as iron, cobalt, and nickel, then through some semimetallic elements such as germanium, and on to some nonmetals such as arsenic, selenium, and bromine on the right side of the table.

In general, there is a smooth gradation in chemical and physical properties as a period is crossed, but exceptions to this general rule abound and make the study of chemistry a fascinating and unpredictably complex fi ld.

Tips 5 在元素周期表中可以看到，随着原子序数的增加，化学元素在性质上表现出近似的重复性

The periodic law states that after certain regular but varying intervals the chemical elements show an approximate repetition in their properties. For example, fluorine氟, chlorine氯, and bromine溴, share the property of forming white crystalline salts 白色晶体盐 of general formula NaX with the metal sodium钠.

**This periodic repetition of properties is the essential fact that underlies all aspects
of the periodic system.**（元素性质的周期性重复 是元素周期律所有规律的本质）

Among the elements, however, not only does the period length vary, but also the periodicity is not exact. The elements within any column of the periodic table are not exact recurrences of each other. In this respect, their periodicity is not unlike the musical scale, in which one returns to a note denoted by the same letter, which sounds like the original note but is defi nitely not identical to it, being an octave higher. （周期性像音阶，有周期性，但也不是完全重复，像是音阶升高是，音符重复出现，发音相似，写法重复，但高了八度）。
